# @vendoai/core spec — coverage stress-test findings (pre-freeze review)

**Date:** 2026-07-10 · **Reviews:** 11 parallel block reviews (apps, actions, guard, automations, meter, knowledge, memory, mcp, ui, store, agent)
**Spec under review:** `2026-07-10-vendoai-core-spec.md` (Draft v3) · **Context:** PRD (Open-Source Full-Stack Agentic Interface) + the three research docs on this branch

**Method.** For each block, a dream-feature list (30–48 features, 2–3-year ambition, brainstormed *without* seeing the spec to avoid anchoring) was walked through the core spec with exactly one question per feature: *could core express this, or does it hit a wall?* Not "should we build it" — only "does the foundation forbid it." 503 features tested across 11 blocks. Everything expressible was discarded; what remains is 9 cross-block walls and 11 strain clusters (13 block-level walls and 64 block-level strains, deduplicated — the same few contracts recur everywhere).

**Ranking.** Findings are ordered by how expensive the contract change becomes **after** the freeze — stored-record accumulation (grants, audit rows, seals are forever), security-semantics reversals, and wire/ecosystem breaks rank above additive type changes.

**No spec rewrites are proposed.** Each finding names the contract that is missing or wrong and the decision the spec session owns.

## Part 1 — Walls (features core cannot express), ranked by post-freeze cost

### 1. No delegated authority — a call can never execute under an identity other than the session principal — **expensive-breaking**
- **Contract:** `Principal` (§4) is a closed `user | anonymous` union with no actor/subject split; grants are principal-scoped stored records (§7, §10); `CallProvenance` (§7) has no on-behalf-of; **I3 (§6) states the opposite of delegation verbatim: "a sandbox cannot call on behalf of anyone else."**
- **Blocks/features hit:** apps (Run-As-Me grants; agent-persona standing authority), actions (on-behalf-of impersonation, delegation grants, service accounts, act-as-organization), guard (approval-authority delegation), automations (vacation handoff, offboarding escrow — actor≠subject audit), agent (inbound A2A "bank-agent acting for user X").
- **Why it's a wall:** a manager's "$200 refund" grant is unreachable from a report's session — grant lookup happens against the caller's principal rows; no shape lets a grant minted by principal A attach to calls made by principal B. I3's credential binds the run to one identity, and the actions layer executes "as the signed-in user." The only contortion (owner-owned automation triggered by the other user) misattributes audit: I7 rows record the wrong actor, and the true actor survives only in an untyped trigger payload. Impersonation squeezed through `claims` leaves the dual attribution compliance needs structurally invisible to guard policy and audit.
- **Cost rationale:** not one additive field. It changes the semantics of every stored grant record (whose grants gate a call), the I3 credential binding (an invariant *worded to forbid this*), execution identity at the binding endpoint, and the meaning of `principal` on every audit row — the security argument for I2/I3/I5/I7 must be re-derived. Every grant/audit row minted before the change permanently lacks the actor dimension.

### 2. "Destructive is never suppressible by anything" forbids every standing/derived suppression — **expensive-breaking to reverse; likely deliberate — needs eyes-open ratification**
- **Contract:** §3.1 risk table ("destructive: always asks — no grant, fade, or rule may ever suppress"), restated §7 (parked-card rules; "'Always allow' is not offered for a destructive parked action"; dev-shipped modules included).
- **Blocks/features hit:** guard (progressive trust "auto-approve refunds under $100", downward risk reclassification per context, bounded-window break-glass, quorum-approved *standing* pre-authorization for recurring destructive ops), automations (pre-approved action envelopes covering destructive tools; any nightly job whose normal duty is a destructive call parks every run).
- **Why it's a wall:** each feature terminates in guard answering a destructive call without a fresh human ask on that instance; grants, parked-action resolution, and the ladder each independently hard-code the refusal. This is not a policy-pipeline gap the guard track can fill — core's semantics forbid the verdict.
- **A separable sub-defect (cheap reword if wanted):** §7's "approving a parked action **releases that one instance only**" also forbids **bulk-enumerated release** — a 212-item destructive batch is 212 individual taps; one consent card enumerating and releasing all 212 held, fully-rendered instances (still per-fire, still no standing grant) is currently non-conformant. The suppression absolute may well be the product ("money always needs you"); the one-instance-only wording over-reaches into a shape that *does* keep a human in the loop.
- **Cost rationale:** a published security guarantee woven into grant semantics and the trust story; adding any escape later (quorum-standing grants, break-glass windows) is a semantics reversal. The spec session should consciously accept the 2–3-year enterprise cost, or carve a narrow, loudly-audited exception shape now.

### 3. Single-end-user scoping of all storage and policy — no org/team/tenant/host scope class, and "principal-scoped" is ambiguous — **moderate per change, pervasive; stored rows accumulate wrong from day one**
- **Contract:** §4 + §10: "every storage operation and policy decision is principal-scoped"; `Principal` offers no collective subject (org/team), no host/system scope, no admin/approver actor, no contract for reading another principal's rows; "hosts must never mint fake-permanent subjects" (§4) forecloses the synthetic-principal workaround; and the spec never defines whether "principal-scoped" means *hard data partition* or *authorization context*.
- **Blocks/features hit — the single widest finding of the review (~40 features):**
  - **guard:** the entire enterprise cluster — org/team policy hierarchies, approval chains & quorum (approver ≠ actor must read/answer a request stored under the actor's principal), escalation ladders, approval inbox, org-wide blast-radius counters and kill switches, tenant policy isolation, org analytics/attestation.
  - **meter:** org wallets, pooled quotas, budget delegation/sponsorship, chargeback, host price books, tenant rate cards, global circuit-breakers, fraud clustering, revenue-share ledgers, every cross-principal aggregate read (forecasting, margin, export, what-if replay).
  - **memory:** org/team memory rings, inheritance/overrides, anonymized team pattern mining (an aggregate query across 8 users' memories has *no legal caller shape at all*).
  - **ui:** multiplayer threads / human handoff / live shared replays — a thread has no participants dimension; user B's read of user A's thread record has no contract-legal path (modules got a share/import contract in §5.3; threads got none).
  - **apps/store:** shared live module state (two principals addressing one instance), org gallery visibility scopes, role-based publish permissions.
  - **automations:** org-level automations acting for many users; team digest composing across N principals' data in one logical run.
- **Why it's a wall:** trace any of them — the record (approval request, pool balance, team fact, shared thread, org counter) either belongs to no principal, spans principals, or must be read by a different principal than its owner. Under the stated rule each of those operations is contractually unreachable; `claims` can carry roles into *policy evaluation* but the *storage* rule has no other scope.
- **Cost rationale:** each fix is additive in code (scope classes on records, participant/ACL dimensions, admin-read operations, a collective Principal arm) — but it amends a blanket security rule that `withAnonymousGuard` and I7 are being built against; the strict-vs-loose "principal-scoped" ruling must land **before** `@vendoai/store` bakes one reading into implementations; and audit/grant/meter rows minted before the amendment permanently lack the scope dimensions. Also note the PRD promises "sharing like Google Docs" while §5.3 defines sharing as import-a-copy — quietly two different models; one clarifying sentence decides which.

### 4. GDPR erasure and retention collide with I7's absolute append-only audit; crypto-shredding is now-or-never — **moderate for the invariant; effectively unfixable later for the keying decision**
- **Contract:** I7 (§6): audit rows must carry principal + provenance + seal; "append-only"; "no configuration can disable audit." No redaction arm, tombstone-vs-content distinction, retention class, legal-hold precedence, or crypto-shred carve-out exists on any slice (§10). Audit rows also carry exact arguments (§7's card rule), i.e. user content.
- **Blocks/features hit:** store (one-call GDPR erasure across slices, crypto-shredding, retention/legal hold, BYO-KMS revocation), memory (right-to-erasure while "remember X" tool calls sit verbatim in an unpurgeable log; incognito retroactive strike), guard (SIEM export with PII stripping, compliance-pack retention, "forget everything" — plus the inverse defect: `withAnonymousGuard` routes anonymous *audit writes* to the TTL-reaped ephemeral layer, so security evidence from the most abuse-prone principal class structurally evaporates).
- **Why it's a wall:** a conformant implementation must refuse the erasure; a GDPR-compliant one must violate the invariant. And crypto-shredding requires every row to be envelope-encrypted under a per-user key **from the first production row** — it cannot be retrofitted onto plaintext rows, backups, or replicas.
- **Cost rationale:** re-scoping I7 (protect audit *structure* — append-only, tombstone-preserving, event-complete — while permitting principal-keyed content redaction and declared retention classes) is a moderate invariant amendment; the per-principal keying decision is the one item in this review that is effectively **now or never**.

### 5. CallProvenance cannot represent the external (MCP) door — **moderate, but must land with or before the mcp block ships, defeating promote-when-needed**
- **Contract:** `CallProvenance` (§7): `kind` closed to `"chat" | "module" | "automation"`, only `moduleId?/seal?/origin?` fields, no `meta` extension point (unlike `ToolDescriptor`); I2 (§6) enumerates exactly two stamping boundaries (runtime outside sandbox; `toToolSet` in the route handler) and rejects unstamped calls.
- **Blocks/features hit:** mcp (12 features — per-client policy/subsetting, external-call audit ledger, per-surface rate limits, metered external AI tier, anomaly baselines per user×client, connected-agents console, emergency brake, agent-mesh attribution), knowledge (directions scoped "in-product only, not MCP"; per-client quotas), guard/meter (external traffic indistinguishable from first-party chat in policy and billing — §15's own meter sketch uses `provenance.kind` as the billing dimension).
- **Why it's a wall:** an inbound ChatGPT `tools/call` resolves a Principal fine (§4 fits), but there is no defined I2 boundary for the MCP endpoint, no truthful `kind`, and no field for OAuth client identity — the block must stamp a lie ("chat"), and every append-only audit/meter row records external traffic as embedded chat, permanently.
- **Cost rationale:** the type change is additive (new kind + optional client/chain fields) but I2 needs a third named boundary (security semantics), every `kind` consumer needs a fail-closed rule for unknown kinds, and misattributed stored rows are forever. The PRD ships an mcp block, so this is guaranteed to be hit at 0.3.0-era, not in 2 years.

### 6. No legal provenance for a turn the user didn't just send — detached/background/agent-initiated execution — **moderate**
- **Contract:** same shapes as #5: `CallProvenance.kind` + I2's boundary enumeration + §3.2's per-request binding; plus §7's attended/unattended fork (park vs prompt) has no input bit saying "nobody is watching," and I3 credentials cover module runs only.
- **Blocks/features hit:** agent (background continuation after tab close, ambient/proactive triggers, standing objectives, the resumed leg of human-gate checkpoints), actions (durable sagas/bulk jobs resumed days later that are neither chat nor module), memory (block-initiated prefetch/janitor writes have no legal provenance category).
- **Why it's a wall:** a server worker continuing a chat turn has no route handler (so `chat` contradicts I2's definition and guard treats calls as attended — a `write` "asks" with nobody to ask), no module/seal (so `automation`'s grant machinery can't attach), and honest no-provenance is rejected by I2. The guard rule feature-8-style products need ("what may run unattended") cannot be written over core shapes.
- **Cost rationale:** additive in data terms (a kind or an `execution: attended|unattended` dimension), but it edits I2's boundary enumeration, the §7 fork, grant context scope, and needs an I3-style credential story for detached workers — it re-opens the reviewed invariant set. Alternatively, ratifying "every durable/unattended job IS a module" (the one-artifact thesis) closes actions' half of this cleanly — but not the continued-chat-turn half.

### 7. Superseded seals fail closed — no multi-version concurrency, no consent-free rollback — **moderate, trending expensive once hosts rely on the current promise**
- **Contract:** §5.1 ("any envelope security edit, any repo change, any granted-tool descriptor drift lapses the grants and re-prompts") + I8 (stale/superseded seal must fail closed) + §5.2's single-current-seal lineage.
- **Blocks/features hit:** automations (canary rollout with 90% on the old version, shadow mode, one-click rollback to a previously-approved seal), apps (the same lifecycle for high-iteration modules).
- **Why it's a wall:** creating the canary mints seal₂, which lapses seal₁'s grants at edit time; nothing distinguishes "previously-human-approved lineage member" from the TOCTOU mutation I8 actually targets, so rollback to seal₁ requires a fresh consent ceremony and the old version cannot keep firing. The clone-to-new-module workaround forfeits grant lineage and shared run history.
- **Cost rationale:** no stored-record migration (grants already carry seals) — but the fix rewrites the *meaning* of a security invariant ("edit lapses grants" → "…except approved historical seals per lineage policy"), on which consent cards, conformance tests, and the anti-retiming rationale all rest.

### 8. Tool execution is single-shot — no progress/streaming/multi-emission arm — **moderate**
- **Contract:** `execute(input, ctx): Promise<ToolOutcome>` resolves exactly once; `ToolOutcome` is `ok | error | pending` with `pending.kind` closed to `"approval" | "parked"` (§3). No shape carries server-initiated events into a turn.
- **Blocks/features hit:** actions (bulk-operation progress, long-running operations, streaming tool outputs the agent acts on mid-stream, GraphQL subscriptions/live queries), agent (streaming tool results, live progress ticker), meter (quota-park/quote-consent doesn't fit either pending kind).
- **Why it's a wall:** the chunk stream has nowhere to go — the framework bridge closes over one promise, the sandbox MCP endpoint speaks request→response, and guard judges once at input-complete. Core's neutral shape is strictly *narrower than the frameworks it wraps* (Vercel's `Tool.execute` accepts `AsyncIterable`); the poll-via-second-tool workaround is a different feature. Smuggling a stream inside `result` skips the guarded pipeline and breaks I7's one-row-per-call accounting.
- **Cost rationale:** type-additive, but every exhaustive `ToolOutcome` consumer, the sandbox MCP wire, and guard's judge-once semantics must handle the new arm (when is a streaming result judged/parked mid-emission?). MCP's experimental tasks SEP (progress notifications already on the wire) is the standards-aligned retrofit path — which also makes it cheaper *now* than after the sandbox endpoint ships.

### 9. Invariant I10, as written, forbids the Directions category — **moderate (wording/trust-class defect in a security invariant)**
- **Contract:** I10 (§6): "any host data entering a module or the generation context is untrusted … **must never be treated as instructions** to the agent or module."
- **Blocks/features hit:** knowledge (company directions as a first-class type, scoped/scheduled directions, steering marketplaces — the PRD names "company directions" as half the knowledge block's purpose).
- **Why it's a wall:** a direction ("never promise delivery dates") is host-authored content retrieved per-turn and injected into the generation context *explicitly to be obeyed* — verbatim what I10 prohibits; §6 makes invariants conformance requirements. I10's intent is prompt-injection defense for anchor-data and runtime business data, but its text draws the boundary at "host data" rather than "operator-authored steering vs. runtime data." Third-party direction packs sharpen it into a genuine gap: core has no trust class under which any non-system-prompt content may legitimately instruct the agent.
- **Cost rationale:** no stored records or wire move — but it's a security invariant other tracks' conformance tests build against; re-scoping after freeze is a security re-review, not an edit.

## Part 2 — Strains (expressible but ugly), grouped by root cause; judgment calls flagged

### S1. The approvalSeal hashes an unpinned descriptor subset — every enrichment risks a fleet-wide grant lapse ⚠ *near-wall by timing*
`approvalSeal = hash(buildKey + descriptor hashes of granted tools)` (§5.1), but the spec never pins **which descriptor fields** enter the hash. A nightly description-enrichment pass, a `formats` tweak, or a new `meta.compensator` field (the sanctioned extension point!) lapses every grant on those tools product-wide though behavior never changed — the exact hazard §5.1 already fixed for *caches* (buildKey) and §5.4 fixed for sync commits, left open on the security side. Tool versioning/deprecation and "re-prompt only on semantic change" are unbuildable on an all-fields hash. *(actions, agent, apps, mcp — per-client descriptor variants must never become canonical for the same reason.)*
**Judgment call:** pin the canonical seal-bearing subset (name, input/output schema, risk, binding, idempotent — not description/title/formats/meta) **before grants and seals persist**. Redefining the hash later is a one-time global grant-lapse event; the trade (description-only drift will not re-prompt) must be accepted consciously.

### S2. Seal-lapse absolutism has no benign-change tier — consent churn taxes every editor and self-evolving module
A drag-resize, display-name edit, or notification-target change — security surface byte-identical — lapses every grant and demands the ceremony (§5.1 is absolute by design). Visual editors imply dozens of re-prompts per session; an agent may never self-tune an automation that holds write grants. *(apps, automations.)*
**Judgment call:** ratify "every edit re-prompts" as the product stance and put diff-aware re-consent on guard's roadmap, or rule now whether guard may auto-re-mint when the *derived security surface* (granted-tool hashes + egress + trigger + secrets) is hash-identical — §5.1's current wording forecloses that relaxation, and deciding later reinterprets what existing approvals meant.

### S3. CallProvenance is flat and correlation-blind — no runId/threadId/sessionRef/surface, no parent chain, no meta slot
Ten features per block keep hitting the same four missing optional fields: automation A triggers B triggers C and audit can't say "because A"; run id exists at stamping time (I3 binds it into the credential) but never surfaces; every surface (palette, sidebar, voice, canvas) collapses to `kind:"chat"`; agent-computed facts (confidence, taint score, simulation flag, delegation contract) have no unforgeable carrier to reach guard, so they ride toolCallId side-tables around the very shape I2 makes trustworthy. *(automations, guard, meter, ui, memory, agent, actions.)*
**Judgment call:** add optional `runId`/`threadId`/`surface`/`parent`/namespaced `meta` (stamped only at I2 boundaries) now, or accept that append-only audit history before the promotion is permanently uncorrelatable and the closed `kind` enum needs widening discipline. Cheap now; lossy forever later.

### S4. Grants have a module scope dimension but no surface/client dimension — in-product "always allow" silently extends to every OAuth'd external client
§7's precedent (chat grants don't auto-suppress in shared modules) implies surface should scope grants, but the shape has no dimension for it, and can't reference client identity until finding #5 gives provenance one. *(mcp, guard.)*
**Judgment call:** default-open or default-closed at the MCP door — one sentence now; a security migration over already-minted grant records later.

### S5. The parked/pending call has no durable record, owner, or resolution contract
`ToolOutcome.pending.ref` "correlates the eventual resume" — but no core shape holds the parked call, no §10 slice stores it, and no resolve-ref API exists. Cross-surface approval (park at the MCP door, approve in the in-product inbox) makes it a two-block shape — by §1's own admission rule, core territory. Long-lived out-of-band approvals (a wire transfer awaiting a manager for two days) must outlive the framework turn and be answered by a non-actor; server-computed previews (preflight quotes, affected-row counts) have no payload slot and ride an unspecified ref-indirection to the consent card. *(mcp, guard, actions, agent.)*
**Judgment call:** name the owner (guard as single park-state owner that mcp/actions write through, or a core slice) and bless "parked" as the general durable arm for interactive calls too — before two blocks improvise incompatible park stores around security-adjacent state (resolving a ref releases a held write).

### S6. Block-isolation leaves recurring cross-block seams uncontracted — the "shape two blocks must agree on" test is already met in five places
- **Context contribution to the agent's turn** (knowledge directions, memory retrieval/briefings — neither can reach prompt assembly; the umbrella is contractually behavior-free, so the composed e2e product can't wire it either) — the seam is needed by two blocks on day one, three with guard's judge context.
- **Observation/events out** (memory's ambient learning has no data source: threads are opaque *by deliberate ruling with the promotion path foreclosed* — promoting the ai-SDK message shape would violate core's no-framework non-goal — so mining needs an agent-side neutral-transcript projection; guard's DLP/taint needs a `ToolOutcome.meta` channel and stream/egress-proxy hooks that don't exist; evals/experiments need a sampling surface that isn't the undisableable audit log).
- **Usage/instrumentation** (meter can wrap tool calls, but LLM tokens live inside agent and sandbox minutes inside runtime — `RunResult` carries no duration/usage — so metering beyond tool calls is voluntary per-block emission; five blocks depending on a meter-owned interface is §1's definition of a core shape).
- **An LLM provider handle for non-agent blocks** (guard's judge, knowledge's query understanding, memory's extraction each ship their own key wiring — fragmenting the locked "one key" OSS story; a neutral config-holding inference-handle shape breaches no non-goal).
- **Server→surface delivery** (Channels was evicted to automations, but §7's parked-action notification is a **v1 flow**: automations emits, ui renders — the two-block condition is met at 0.3.0, not in the future).
- **Lifecycle gates at save/publish/run** (meter entitlement checks, review/eval/a11y gates — only guard's grant-mint beat exists in §5.3; everything else becomes uncontracted host glue, differently per install).
**Judgment call per seam:** reserve the shape now (the `memory?`/`meter?` slice reservation is the in-spec precedent) or explicitly schedule the promotion — the risk is each owning track freezing a hook shape the consuming block can't satisfy without a dependency, then re-releasing the whole version train.

### S7. Anonymous→authenticated continuation has no sanctioned hook
`withAnonymousGuard`'s ephemerality is exactly right until sign-in: claim-on-login must race the TTL reaper, the post-login op arrives under a `user` principal the guard won't route, and a transplant through public slice APIs violates "callers never author identity." Grants must never transplant (that would launder least-privilege scrutiny) — data might. *(meter, memory, store.)*
**Judgment call:** a consent-gated `claimSession` affordance on the wrapper (decided before the store track implements it — the wrapper's internal layout determines whether claim is possible at all), or document read-then-rewrite as the blessed dance.

### S8. The runtime interface is missing four seams the blocks already reach for
- **Headless/static render** — `render()` is a live browser view, `execute()` is service code; "materialize this module's UI at 8am as PDF/image/email-HTML with no user session" is neither; a static render must be explicitly *not* an MCP Apps render (no capability mint, no state writes). *(apps, ui.)*
- **Interrupt/kill** — `execute()` is fire-to-completion; the only cancellation in core is the per-call AbortSignal, so a kill switch can starve future calls but cannot stop a burning run. *(guard.)*
- **Serve-outward compile seam** — serving modules to ChatGPT needs the compiled single-HTML artifact (exists, cached by buildKey, no contract surface) and an I9 analog for renders Vendo doesn't host; §9's "degrades read-only in ChatGPT" wording reads as a ceiling that would forbid stateful-via-proxied-app-tools, which is standard-conformant. *(mcp.)*
- **Platform-neutral gloss** — `ModuleView { element; dispose() }` and I9's mechanism language are browser-shaped; tier-1 (view.json) is genuinely portable to SwiftUI/Compose *if* the schema stays free of web-isms; tier-2 is web-by-definition (§5.2) — a deliberate bet worth stating once so native runtimes conform rather than fork. *(ui, apps.)*
All additive; flagged so the runtime track doesn't harden the interface with a human-attached browser window assumed everywhere.

### S9. Storage contracts: four rulings the store track needs before implementations calcify
- **ModuleStorage's unit of exchange is unspecified while git is baked into security keys** — if the slice contract turns out to be "return a filesystem path to a bare repo," every non-filesystem backend (Mongo/Dynamo/browser-OPFS/KV) is retroactively broken; if it's "get/put bundle/packfile bytes" (runtime materializes), every backend works. Pin bytes-not-paths, and state that the git substrate is a permanent contract.
- **Cross-slice consistency is undefined where I7/I8 imply atomicity** — I8 survives via comparison-at-use (no cross-slice txn needed — worth *stating*), but a crash between the Redis grant write and the Postgres audit row yields a granted permission with no audit trail and no contract saying which side fails; multi-region CAS silently requires a linearizable grants domain. One paragraph of ordering prose; conformance kits have nothing normative to test until it exists.
- **The registry has no slots for known-coming slices and no metadata** — §5.6 lists "big assets" as slice-stored with no slice to hold them; knowledge (a PRD block) has no reserved key while memory/meter do; ui has no preferences/attachments home (v1 chrome persistence becomes abuse-of-`state` or bespoke config that breaks the one-`database` story). Reserving `knowledge?`/`blobs?`/`vectors?`/`preferences?` is one line each.
- **Changefeed as a named optional conformance capability** — ordering/replay can't be bolted onto adapters designed without CDC; the adapter ecosystem certified pre-promotion would need a second certification tier.

### S10. Envelope corners: three fields sit in the wrong place or outside the signature
- **Fork lineage lives inside `anchor`** (§5.5) — a fork of a non-anchored module has no lineage home; lineage graphs would squat in a page-placement field. Trivially separable now; a stored-record migration later. *(apps.)*
- **Module commercial terms** can't live in the sealed envelope (a price change would lapse grants — a security ceremony for a commercial edit) and the side-table alternative sits *outside* the §5.3 share signature — the one thing import verifies fail-closed doesn't cover the money terms the receiver agrees to. *(meter.)*
- **Composition and the seal** — I9 rejects nested frames (so embedding is host-composed sibling iframes — fine), but `approvalSeal` doesn't cover embedded modules' seals: "A embeds B, B drifts" lapses nothing on A. Whether transitive deps ever enter the seal is a security-semantics decision the spec session should own. *(apps.)*

### S11. Smaller one-decision items
- **ThemeTokens is a fixed vocabulary with unspecified extensibility** — motion/density/custom tokens have no slot; the cheapest change in the whole doc (theme is presentation-only), silence just breeds incompatible preset formats. *(ui.)*
- **Offline** — queued writes overload `pending{kind:"parked"}` (designed for approval holds); `vendo/state` has no error/queue semantics, so offline module writes are silently lost. Declare offline out-of-contract-scope or reserve intent semantics. *(apps, ui.)*
- **Policy code escape hatch** — guard's per-call policy hook must masquerade as a dev-shipped module to reuse the sandbox; entry point shaped wrong for a hot path (the contemplated isolate middle tier fits). *(guard.)*
- **MCP resources/prompts** — no core shape, no risk label for a resource read, I7 doesn't mandate a row for it; the wrap-as-read-tool contortion works — state whether it's the blessed pattern or non-tool egress falls under guard. *(mcp, knowledge next.)*
- **Cross-host federation** — the MCP door is a workable read-across story; identity *linking* between a user's two accounts and cross-host provenance have no shape. Bless MCP-mediated federation explicitly so nobody designs a bespoke Vendo↔Vendo protocol that fights the grants model. *(apps, agent.)*
- **Neutral transcript projection** — conversation mining (knowledge) and ambient learning (memory) need a framework-free `{role, text, ts}` view agents can emit; decide before the agent track freezes its persistence shape. *(knowledge, memory.)*

**What did *not* break — worth knowing at triage.** Reviewers repeatedly noted features passing *because of* the spec's boldest choices: module-as-git-repo makes history/forking/dedup/portability nearly free; the emergent capability tiers, injected view-renderer, and live-CSS-variable theming survived every apps/ui probe; `ToolOutcome`'s pending arm and seal-bound grants with I5 argument constraints carried most of the automations dream list; the slice registry's `Pick` pattern and anonymous-as-first-class held everywhere; `Principal.tenantId`+`claims` covered RLS, residency routing, and permission-trimmed retrieval unmodified; and nothing anywhere required changing the MCP Apps bet or the one-version-train. The walls are concentrated in exactly four load-bearing shapes — Principal, CallProvenance, the seal lifecycle, and I7/I10's absolutes — which is what makes them cheap to rule on now and expensive to renegotiate later.

**Recurring meta-pattern.** Most strains share one root cause: core's shapes are all request-scoped and single-principal, while roughly a third of the dream list is about *crossing* those lines — other people (collaboration, delegation, org), other times (background, versions, replay), other surfaces (MCP door, native, email), other blocks (context in, events out).
